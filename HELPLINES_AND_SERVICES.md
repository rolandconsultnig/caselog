## 🕐 WHAT TO EXPECT

### When You Call a Helpline

**Step 1: Initial Contact (5 minutes)**
- Trained counselor answers
- You explain your situation
- Counselor assesses immediate danger
- Emergency services dispatched if needed

**Step 2: Assessment (10-15 minutes)**
- Counselor asks questions
- Your needs are identified
- Services are explained
- Referrals are made

**Step 3: Action Plan (10 minutes)**
- Immediate safety plan
- Medical referral (if needed)
- Legal referral (if needed)
- Shelter referral (if needed)
- Follow-up scheduled

**Step 4: Follow-up**
- Check-in calls
- Service coordination
- Progress tracking
- Continued support

### When You Visit a Hospital

**Step 1: Arrival**
- Private consultation room
- Female doctor (if preferred)
- Confidential registration

**Step 2: Medical Examination (30-60 minutes)**
- Medical history
- Physical examination
- Forensic evidence collection
- Injury documentation
- Photographs (with consent)

**Step 3: Treatment (varies)**
- Wound care
- Medications
- PEP (within 72 hours)
- Emergency contraception (within 120 hours)
- STI prophylaxis
- Tetanus shot

**Step 4: Documentation**
- Medical report
- Police medical form
- Discharge instructions
- Follow-up appointments

**Step 5: Referrals**
- Counseling services
- Legal services
- Police (if desired)
- Support groups

## 🚨 SAFETY TIPS

### If You're in Immediate Danger
1. **Call 112** (Police Emergency)
2. **Get to a safe place**
3. **Don't confront the perpetrator**
4. **Preserve evidence** (don't wash, change clothes)
5. **Seek medical help within 72 hours**

### If You're Safe Now
1. **Call SGBV helpline** (0800-033-3333)
2. **Seek medical care**
3. **Consider reporting to police**
4. **Get counseling**
5. **Tell someone you trust**
